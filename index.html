Introduction

This project is done in the frame of the course of Data visualization. It has for purpose to show the different elements that can influence the vote of the French population through vizualisations. The time boundaries of this project is from 2002 to 2017, being the last presidential election.

"Opinion is something intermediary between knowledge and ignorance"
- Republic, Plato

This quote is the basis of our work, we hope through these vizualisation to spread objective political knowledge and erase different preconceived information. We will explore through the following information key information about the different influences that can touch the votes of the French citizen. Every data comes from a governmental source and is therefore not subject to controversy or discussion.

Chronology

Firstly, to understand a little bit more the French population mindset, it seems important to focus on its environment and on the different events that occurred since 2002. Of course, a lot happened in 15 years, but we selected the main events that have the power to influence the vote of a French citizen. Among these we can quote the different terrorist attacks that happened in France in 2015 or the laws affecting minorities (face covering ban, gay marriage…)

Elections Result

Have you ever wondered how the political landscape changed in France among the last years? Some of us may know the latest Frenfch presidents or even the second round. We wanted to explore this matter through a vizualisation exploring the votes at the presidential elections since 2002. The switch in votes of the regions throughout the years or the France fracture that can be observed through the support to the winning candidate on the second turn remain important data to understand the French dynamics.

Regional election

Now that we've understand how the French citizen vote per region and the different events in France that could've oriented their votes, it seems important to explore the correlation between different factors affecting the population and their vote. In this vizualisation, we did some statistical work to understand the profile of the departments voting for each political parti/candidate. For example, departments having an higher revenue will vote for the right wing (here represented by Francois Fillon). We also displayed the statistical significance of the data, since the purpose of the project is to be as objective and clear as possible and to avoid stereotypes.
We also included the white voters and the abstentionist profiles, being two majors political forces in France that kept increasing over the last few years.
